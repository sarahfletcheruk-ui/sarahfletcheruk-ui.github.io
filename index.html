    <section>

      <p>

        Sarah Fletcher is an American-British writer. Her debut collection

        <em>PLUS ULTRA</em> was published by Cheerio Publishing in 2023.

        Her poetry has appeared in <em>The Poetry Review</em>, <em>The White Review</em>,

        <em>Poetry London</em>, and other publications. She has written articles for

        <em>The New Statesman</em>, <em>The Washington Post</em>, and <em>The Critic</em>.

        She is completing a PhD at Aberystwyth University on chronic pain and language.

        She has published fiction in <em>The London Magazine</em> and is currently editing a novel.

      </p>


      <p>

        She is interested in writing both critically and creatively about medicine,

        psychiatry, gender, pop culture, poetry, and film.

      </p>


      <p>

        <strong>Available for commissions, editing, teaching, talks, and workshops.</strong>

      </p>

      <h2>Praise</h2>


      <blockquote>

        <p>

          “By turns evocative of English landscape paintings and frantic late-night conversations,

          Sarah Fletcher’s poems are highly mobile, troubled, troubling, rich and fraught.”

        </p>

        <footer>— Chris Kraus, author of <em>I Love Dick</em></footer>

      </blockquote>


      <blockquote>

        <p>

          “<em>PLUS ULTRA</em> is an alarming, accomplished and brilliant first collection.

          Fletcher’s ambition, formal invention, erudition and technical precision offer a compelling

          scaffold for poems that fizz and flit between vulnerability, wit and a brutal willingness

          to confront the world, and others, with a dark honesty that is frequently refreshing and,

          occasionally, genuinely menacing.”

        </p>

        <footer>— Ahren Warner</footer>

      </blockquote>


      <blockquote>

        <p>

          “<em>PLUS ULTRA</em> is a wild, exciting, lyrical book, free-associative and narrative

          at the same time … Fletcher is a poet of the future, and lucky for us, she’s arrived just in time.”

        </p>

        <footer>— Matthew Dickman</footer>

      </blockquote>

      <h2>Teaching</h2>

      <p>

        Sarah Fletcher has over a decade of teaching experience. She has taught undergraduates at

        Aberystwyth University and the University of Bath, and has organised writing workshops with

        The Poetry School, The Steiner House, the Writing Squad, and more. She also works in primary

        schools and holds a registered DBS. She has organised a six-part course on pain management

        based on her academic research.
